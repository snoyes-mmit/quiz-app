Our assessments are designed to measure applied knowledge and practical understanding of key concepts relevant to your role or training program.

Each quiz includes 20 multiple-choice questions selected to cover core topics and commonly encountered scenarios. Questions are structured to evaluate not only factual knowledge but also the ability to apply that knowledge effectively.

The goal of these assessments is to provide a clear and reliable snapshot of competency, helping both individuals and organizations understand current skill levels.

Department Assessments  (click below)

Product Assessments

The Product assessment evaluates knowledge related to product development, planning, and cross-functional collaboration. Questions focus on areas such as product strategy, requirements definition, prioritization, user experience considerations, and working with engineering teams. This assessment measures the ability to translate business needs and user insights into clear product direction.

Configuration Assessments

The Configuration assessment measures understanding of system setup, customization, and environment management. Questions focus on configuration best practices, platform settings, workflow setup, and maintaining system consistency across environments. This quiz is designed to evaluate a candidate’s ability to configure systems effectively while supporting operational stability and scalability.

Frontend Development Assessments

The Frontend Development assessment evaluates knowledge of modern web development practices used to build user-facing applications. Questions focus on core concepts such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, responsive design, performance, and common development workflows. This quiz is designed to measure a developer’s ability to create accessible, maintainable, and user-friendly interfaces.

Quality Assurance Assessments

The Quality Assurance assessment measures understanding of testing principles, defect management, and software quality practices. Questions cover topics such as test planning, test case design, regression testing, automation fundamentals, and bug reporting. The goal of this assessment is to evaluate a candidate’s ability to identify issues, ensure reliability, and support the delivery of high-quality software.
